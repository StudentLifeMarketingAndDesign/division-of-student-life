What is PICK ONE!?

PICK ONE! is a University of Iowa initiative that encourages first year, first semester students to get involved in one meaningful campus activity.

How do I make my PICK ONE! selection?

By logging in with your Hawk ID and password and following the listed instructions on the PICK ONE! website.

What are the benefits of PICK ONE!?

There are many benefits to getting involved. They include making new friends, building a strong resume, developing time management skills, becoming connected to the campus community, and achieving better academic success.

When is the PICK ONE! registration deadline?

The PICK ONE! registration deadline is October 1, 2011.

Am I eligible to win prizes if I register my PICK ONE! selection?

Yes, this year we are giving away an Apple iPad and Iowa Hawkeye gear. To be eligible you must make your PICK ONE! selection by October 1, 2011.

Where can I find opportunities to get involved?

Students will discover multiple involvement opportunities during ON IOWA! before classes begin. Also, during the first week of classes (Welcome Back Week) students are encouraged to check out engagement opportunities at the Campus Job Fair (8/23), Student Organization Fair (8/24), and the Volunteer Fair (8/31). Students can also use the PICK ONE! website to explore hundreds of involvement opportunities. If you have questions about student involvement on campus email the Center for Student Involvement and Leadership at getinvolved@uiowa.edu.

Picking One Thing: Getting Started

Picking one thing is easy. In the menu to the right, you'll see a bunch of categories that contain activities and student organizations that can potentially be your "one." You can be as specific or as broad as you want with your one. You can choose a specific activity or just a category (Example: Employment)as your one.

In short: if you see this a button labeled "pick one" then the page you're on is Pickable.

Picking One Thing That's Outside of This Website

We've compiled an exhaustive list of student organizations and activites on this website, but sometimes an organization or activity's website is more informative and provides a clearer idea of what they're about. For example, these sites might have event calendars, blog posts, or image galleries that describe themselves in greater depth than our site.

Sometimes you'll find links to these websites while exploring the Pick One website. When you click on these links, you'll be taken to the organization or activity's website, but you'll see a blue bar at the top of your screen with a "pick this one" button.

After looking at the organization or activity's website, if you decide you want it as your pick, simply click "pick this one." If not, either close the website's tab, or click the pick one logo to return to the Pick One website to continue browsing.
